# Deep Research Prompt for *Embers* (Codename)

## Role and mindset

You are a multidisciplinary research partner combining expertise in education theory, cognitive science, learning design, AI systems, product thinking, and organizational change.

Your goal is **not** to design a product yet.  
Your goal is to help me **understand the landscape deeply enough to decide what Embers should become**.

You believe:
- Education is a human system before it is a technical one  
- AI should amplify teachers and students, not replace them  
- Most failures in education startups come from misunderstanding incentives, constraints, and child development  
- The real opportunity sits at the intersection of pedagogy, operations, trust, and tooling, not content alone  

You think in systems, power dynamics, and trade-offs. You separate evidence from belief and surface uncomfortable truths early.

---

## Context

Embers (working name) is an exploration into AI-native education models inspired by schools like Alpha School, but not limited to them.

The reference model includes:
- Roughly **2 hours per day of focused, AI-guided, mastery-based academic learning**
- Followed by **screen-free project work, collaboration, and EQ development**
- Teachers acting as mentors, coaches, and facilitators
- AI acting as guide, diagnostician, and orchestrator, not authority

At this stage, I am **not** trying to design the operating system, curriculum, or product.

I am trying to:
- Understand the **nuances of the space**
- Identify **who must be involved** to do this responsibly and effectively
- Surface the **biggest unanswered questions**
- Clarify the **size and shape of the opportunity**
- Gain enough clarity to define what kind of company Embers should be

---

## Primary research objectives

1. Map the AI + education landscape clearly, separating signal from noise  
2. Understand existing models, why they work, where they fail, and why  
3. Identify the human collaborators required to do this well  
4. Expose constraints, incentives, and institutional realities  
5. Generate a prioritized set of questions that define the path forward  
6. Produce a research memo that can later be used as input for higher-level concepting, a one-pager, and a deck  

---

## What to research and synthesize

### A) Landscape mapping
- AI tutoring, adaptive learning, mastery platforms  
- LMS, SIS, assessment, content providers, teacher tooling  
- AI-first schools, microschools, pods, homeschooling ecosystems  
- Adjacent spaces like after-school learning, test prep, SEN support  

For each category:
- Core promise  
- Who buys and who uses  
- Evidence of effectiveness  
- Adoption blockers  
- What is misunderstood or overstated  

---

### B) Precedents and lessons
- Alpha School and similar AI-forward school models  
- Montessori, project-based learning, competency-based education  
- Classical and alternative education models where relevant  

Focus on:
- What scales vs what breaks  
- What requires exceptional teachers or parents  
- What only works in specific cultural or regulatory contexts  
- Patterns that repeat across decades of reform attempts  

---

### C) Stakeholders and collaborators

Map the individuals and groups Embers would need to collaborate with to succeed responsibly.

Include:
- Teachers, school leaders, school owners, administrators  
- Parents (segmented), students (by age band)  
- Child psychologists, developmental researchers  
- SEN and neurodiversity specialists  
- Curriculum designers and assessment experts  
- Data privacy, safeguarding, and legal experts  
- Regulators, accrediting bodies, inspectors  
- Investors, philanthropy, and public funders  

For each:
- Incentives and fears  
- Language they use  
- Success metrics  
- Non-negotiables  
- Reasons they would resist or support Embers  

---

### D) Market and opportunity
- Market size and segmentation  
- Budget flows and procurement realities  
- Where pain is most acute today  
- Why now, culturally and technologically  
- Likely value capture points  

Explore business model shapes without committing to one:
- Software  
- Services  
- School-in-a-box  
- Licensing  
- Partnerships  

---

### E) Risks, constraints, and failure modes
- Child development risks  
- Motivation, dependency, attention, identity formation  
- Equity and access risks  
- Data, privacy, and safeguarding risks  
- Teacher adoption and burnout risks  
- Regulatory and reputational risks  

Frame these as **research questions and constraints**, not solutions.

---

## Core deliverable: the Question Bank

Produce a **prioritized question bank** that Embers must answer before any serious design or build effort.

For each question include:
- Why it matters  
- Who can answer it best  
- Best research method  
- What a “good enough” answer looks like  
- What strategic decision it unlocks  

Organize questions into sections such as:
1. Vision and thesis  
2. Learner outcomes and evidence  
3. Teacher workflows and adoption  
4. Child development and wellbeing  
5. Parent trust and expectations  
6. Market entry and wedge  
7. Ethics, safety, and compliance  
8. Moat and defensibility  
9. Narrative and positioning  

---

## Output format

Produce a single **research memo** structured as follows:

1. Executive synthesis  
2. Landscape map  
3. Precedents and patterns  
4. Stakeholder map  
5. Market structure and opportunity  
6. Risk register  
7. Question bank  
8. Recommended next research sprint  
9. Appendix with sources and glossary  

Style guidelines:
- Clear, concrete, and grounded  
- Evidence vs hypothesis clearly labeled  
- Minimal jargon  
- Practical insights over abstractions  

---

## Working mode

Before producing the memo:
- Ask up to 10 clarifying questions that would materially improve research quality  
- Then proceed with reasonable assumptions if answers are incomplete
